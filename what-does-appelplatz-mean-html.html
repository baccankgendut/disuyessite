What does Appelplatz mean?

Spread the love             

Answer

Appellplatz (sometimes spelled appelplatz) is a German term that combines the words “roll call” (Appell) and “area” or “place” (platz) to indicate “area” or “placement” (Platz). In English, the term is most often used to describe the site where Nazi concentration camps held their daily roll calls, such as Auschwitz.

One can also wonder, what exactly is a Lagerälteste?

 Lagerälteste (60) are convicts who have been assigned by the SS to monitor their fellow prisoners. Known as a “block elder” or “block senior,” the Blockälteste was in command of a particular block, or barrack. The Lagerälteste was known as the “camp elder” or the “camp senior,” and he was in command of all of the prisoners in the camp as a whole.

What criteria were used in selecting Kapos?

Kapos were selected by the Schutzstaffel (SS) camp guards to assist them in the administration of the camps. Some of the kapos were in charge of other convicts who were subjected to compulsory labour. In the camps, other kapos were responsible for paperwork and record-keeping.

When a Nazi concentration camp detainee has an attractive male child, this is referred to as Pipel (plural Pipels). Pipels were attractive male children who were given special favours or privileges because they maintained a relationship with another detainee who had been granted some authority over other detainees.

What exactly does the term Sonderkommando mean?

Sonderkommandos (German: [z?nd?k?mando], meaning “special unit”) were labour units made up of German Nazi death camp inmates who were assigned to specific tasks. In the Holocaust, they were mostly constituted of convicts, most of whom were Jews, who were coerced into assisting with the disposal of gas chamber victims under fear of their own deaths.
