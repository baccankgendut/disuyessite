Bryan Bartlett “Bart” Starr (born January 9, 1934) is a retired professional American football player and coach who played in the National Football League (NFL). From 1956 until 1971, he served as the quarterback for the Green Bay Packers, during which time he became the first quarterback in NFL history to lead a club to five championships (1961-1962, 1965-1966-67), as well as Super Bowls I and II. He retired after the 1971 season (at the time, a game between the NFL and AFL champions prior to the league merger which brought AFL teams into the NFL in 1970). 

As the Packers’ head coach, he had a less successful tenure, with a 52–76–3 record throughout the course of his tenure. He was voted the Most Valuable Player of the first two Super Bowls and was selected to four Pro Bowls during his time with the team. During the same year, he was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame, as well as the Green Bay Packers Hall of Fame. In 1966, he was named the league’s Most Valuable Player. To date, no other quarterback in NFL history has a higher playoff passer rating (104.80) than Starr, who has a 9–1 record in the postseason. 

When Starr departed from the NFL in 1972, his career completion percentage of 57.4 was the highest in the league. Through the 2003 season, Starr held the Packers’ franchise record for most games played (196) for a period of 32 years, which included the 1982 and 1983 seasons. Starr attended the University of Alabama from 1952 to 1956, and the Green Bay Packers selected him in the 17th round of the 1956 NFL Draft (200th overall).
