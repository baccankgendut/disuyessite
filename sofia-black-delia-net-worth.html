Early Life and Background

Born on December 24, 1991, in Clifton, New Jersey, Sofia grew up in a household where the arts were appreciated. Her mother, Elinor, works in printing, and her father, Anthony V. D’Elia, is a judge.

With her father’s Italian roots and her mother’s Jewish heritage, Sofia had a rich cultural upbringing. She began acting at the tender age of five and even took up dancing classes at Broadway Bound. This early start was crucial in shaping her future career.

Career Beginnings and Breakthrough Roles

Sofia’s big break came when she landed the role of Bailey Wells on the soap opera All My Children at just 17. This was a significant stepping stone, but it was her role as Tea Marvelli in Skins that really put her on the map.

Playing an openly lesbian teenager, Sofia broke barriers and garnered a lot of attention. Following this, she starred in Gossip Girl as Sage Spence and later appeared in the critically acclaimed The Night Of.

These roles not only showcased her versatility but also significantly boosted her earnings.
