Early Life & Biography

Eddie Van Halen was born on January 26, 1955 in Amsterdam, Netherlands to Jan van Halen and Eugenia van Halen.

His family later moved to California. Eddie and he, along with his brother Alex van Halen started to learn piano at the age of six. Both the brothers have been playing musical instruments from a very young age. He went to Long Beach City College, where he won first place in the annual piano competition from 1964 to 1967.

Later, Eddie started learning how to play the electric guitar. As a teenager, he used to lock himself in his room and practice guitar for hours. He and his brother formed a music band, “The Broken Combs,” along with three other of his friends. They used to perform at the Hamilton Elementary School in Pasadena at lunchtime.

Career

Eddie Van Halen started his career in the year 1972 when he formed the music band, Van Halen, with his brother Alex Van Halen. Together, they used to play at various well-known clubs such as Whisky a Go-Go, and many others.

In the year 1977, they received a recording contract from Warner Bros. Records. Their self-titled music album “Van Halen” reached number 19 in the Billboard pop music charts and thus became one of rock’s most commercially successful debuts. The album went five-times platinum after a year of release.

As of 2019, the Van Halen band is the 20th on the list of best-selling artists in the United States by the Recording Industry Association of America. Additionally, the band has also ranked 13 in the history of Billboard’s Mainstream Rock chart. The band is also ranked 7th in the list of top 100 hard rock artists of all time by VH1.

Awards & Achievements

Eddie Van Halen has won several awards and achievements in his illustrious career. In 2007, he and his brother were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. His single “Eruption” was voted number two in the poll of the “100 Greatest Guitar Solos”.

For his album “ For Unlawful Carnal Knowledge,” he won the 1992 Grammy Award in the category of Best Hard Rock Performance with Vocal.

He also has several patents to his name. He is the lead inventor of patent number US 4656917, US 7183475, and D388117.
