Early Life

Daniel Dwayne Simmons III, better known as “Diggy Simmons” is the son of Joseph and Justine Simmons. He was born on 21 March 1995, in Queens, New York. His grandfather is Daniel Simmons.

He was influenced to music by Wu-Tang Clan, Jay Z, Kanye West, Nas, A Tribe Called Quest, and Lupe Fiasco. He has also preferred bands like Passion Pit, Arctic Monkeys, and Maroon 5.

Career

In 2009, Diggy Simmons released the mixtape called ‘The First Flight’. Over 100,000 people from his blog downloaded it. He gained wider fame after that and was approached by some of the famous recording labels. He decided to choose Atlantic Records and signed a contract with them.

Simmons uploaded a video of the song “Made You Lock” by Nas in 2010. It became a viral video, and he went on to receive critical appreciation from various personalities, including Kanye West. He then uploaded the video of the song “Oh Yeah!”.

In 2010, Simmons released his second mixtape, ‘Airborne’. It included the single “Great Expectations” which was used in the commercial of AT&T. Later, he came up with the mixtape entitled ‘Past Presents Future’.

He then released his debut studio album called ‘Unexpected Arrival’ in 2012. It received critical appreciation and also featured other artists like Jadakiss, Tank, and Jeremih. The album peaked at No. 2 of the US Rap Albums.

Simmons had also released the EP ‘Out of This World’ in 2017. He has released singles like “Copy, Paste”, “Do It Like You”, “88”, “Can’t Relate”, “4 Letter Word”, “My Girl”, and “Ain’t Bout to Do”. He has performed as a featured artist for Mindless Behavior and Dionne Bromfield.

Awards & Achievements

In 2011, Diggy Simmons got nominated for the BET Hip Hop Awards. He is the winner of the 2012 NAACP Image Awards in the category of “Outstanding New Artist”. In 2007, he was nominated for the Teen Choice Awards. He is also the winner of the BET Awards in 2012. He has appeared in the annual list by XXL. He has also appeared in the “artist to watch” by Billboard in 2011.
